I love Birds

India is home to a wide variety of bird species, ranging from majestic eagles and vultures to colorful parrots and kingfishers. Here are some of the most notable bird species found in India:

Great Indian Bustard

Great Indian Bustard: This large ground bird is found in the grasslands and scrublands of India, but has suffered a significant population decline due to habitat loss and hunting. It is listed as critically endangered by the IUCN and is one of the most endangered bird species in India.

Forest Owlet

Forest Owlet: This small, elusive owl is found only in the forests of central India, and is critically endangered due to habitat loss and fragmentation. It is one of the rarest owl species in the world, with fewer than 250 individuals estimated to remain in the wild.

Jerdon's Courser

Jerdon's Courser: This small, nocturnal bird is found in the scrublands of Andhra Pradesh and is critically endangered due to habitat loss and hunting. The total population is estimated to be fewer than 250 individuals, making it one of the rarest birds in the world.

White-Bellied Heron

White-bellied Heron: This large, striking bird is found in the rivers and wetlands of northeast India, but is critically endangered due to habitat loss and disturbance, as well as hunting and fishing practices. The total population is estimated to be fewer than 250 individuals.

Sociable Lapwing

Sociable Lapwing: This migratory bird is found in the grasslands and wetlands of northern India, but is critically endangered due to habitat loss and degradation, as well as hunting and disturbance. The total population is estimated to be fewer than 1,000 individuals.
